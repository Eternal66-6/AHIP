1. Project Identity

Project Name: AHIP – AI Healthcare Intelligence Platform

Tagline: From fragmented healthcare workflows to coordinated intelligence-driven action.

AHIP is an Agentic AI Healthcare Operations Intelligence Platform. It is designed to support healthcare payer and healthcare operations teams by observing workflows, detecting gaps, coordinating agents, prioritizing risks and recommending operational actions.

2. What AHIP Is Not

  • It is not a chatbot.
  • It is not a generic RAG assistant.
  • It is not a medical diagnosis system.
  • It is not a replacement for EMR, EHR, HRP, FACETS or hospital information systems.
  • It is not intended to make clinical treatment decisions.

3. Enterprise Problem

Healthcare organizations operate across many disconnected workflows: claims, provider contracts, benefit plans, care coordination, documentation, prior authorization, compliance, follow-up, escalation and operational reporting.

Most enterprise systems store data well, but they do not coordinate action well. Teams still depend on manual review, spreadsheets, email follow-ups, tribal knowledge and delayed escalation.

5. AHIP Product Vision

AHIP becomes a healthcare workflow intelligence layer above existing systems. It observes workflow events, builds context, triggers specialized agents, stores memory and recommends next best operational action.

7. Target Customers

  • Healthcare payer operations teams
  • Health plan configuration teams
  • Claims operations teams
  • Provider network and contract teams
  • Compliance and audit teams
  • Hospital operations teams
  • Healthcare SaaS vendors

8. Business Value

  • Reduce manual review effort in healthcare workflow analysis.
  • Detect care, claim and contract workflow gaps earlier.
  • Improve visibility into operational bottlenecks.
  • Provide explainable recommendations instead of raw alerts.
  • Create audit-ready decision trails.
  • Enable healthcare teams to move from reactive handling to proactive workflow intelligence.

9. MVP Definition

The AHIP MVP should demonstrate a realistic healthcare workflow scenario using sample data. It should show how multiple agents analyze patient, claim, provider, benefit and workflow records and produce prioritized, explainable recommendations.

10. MVP Boundaries

Healthcare safety boundary: AHIP must not provide medical diagnosis or clinical treatment recommendations. The MVP focuses on operations, workflow intelligence, claims, benefits, contracts, documentation, care gaps and escalation.
