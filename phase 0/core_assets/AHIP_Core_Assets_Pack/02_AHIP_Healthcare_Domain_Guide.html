1. Why Domain Learning Matters

AHIP is valuable only if it understands real healthcare operations. Interns do not need to become healthcare experts, but they must understand the basic workflows and terminology enough to model agents responsibly.

2. Healthcare Payer Basics

A healthcare payer is an organization that pays or reimburses healthcare claims. Examples include commercial insurers, Medicare-related plans, Medicaid managed care organizations and government-sponsored health plans.

3. Core Domain Entities

4. Claims Lifecycle – Simplified

  1. Patient receives service from provider.
  2. Provider submits claim.
  3. System checks eligibility and benefit coverage.
  4. Contract and pricing rules are applied.
  5. Claim may pass, pend, deny or require manual review.
  6. Payment or explanation is generated.
  7. Exceptions may require documentation, correction or escalation.

5. Provider Contract Workflow

Provider contracts define how providers are reimbursed and under what conditions. Errors in contract configuration can lead to incorrect payments, claim denials, downstream rework or compliance issues.

Common Contract Issues for MVP Scenarios

  • Provider not mapped to correct contract.
  • Contract effective date mismatch.
  • Incorrect network status.
  • Benefit plan and contract mismatch.
  • Missing operational provision.
  • Rate or rule not aligned to claim scenario.

6. Benefit Plan Workflow

Benefit plans define what is covered. For AHIP, the focus is not clinical judgment. The focus is whether workflow data has enough information to validate coverage-related operations.

Benefit Validation Examples

  • Is the patient covered on the service date?
  • Is the service linked to the correct benefit plan?
  • Is prior authorization required?
  • Is there a missing document or review flag?
  • Does the claim need manual review?

7. Care Coordination Workflow

Care coordination ensures follow-up actions happen after healthcare events. In the AHIP MVP, care coordination can be represented through tasks, due dates, workflow status and escalation rules.

8. Compliance and Governance

Healthcare workflows require traceability. AHIP should record why an agent raised an issue, what context was used, what action was recommended, who accepted or overrode it and what happened next.

9. Healthcare Learning Checklist

  • Understand payer vs provider roles.
  • Understand claims lifecycle basics.
  • Understand benefit plan and provider contract meaning.
  • Understand why manual review happens.
  • Understand why audit trail matters.
  • Understand the difference between clinical decisions and operational workflow decisions.
